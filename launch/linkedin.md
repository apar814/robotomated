---

I've been working on something for the past few months, and today I'm sharing it publicly for the first time.

**Robotomated** (robotomated.com) is the intelligence layer for the robotics era.

Here's the problem we're solving:

If you're an operations manager evaluating warehouse robots, you're looking at a $100K+ decision with fragmented information. Manufacturer websites show best-case specs. Review sites are either too narrow or too shallow. And the sales process is designed to sell you their robot, not the right robot.

The same problem exists for facility managers considering cleaning robots, construction supers evaluating inspection drones, and hospital admins looking at pharmacy automation.

**What we built:**

A single platform where you can browse, compare, and get AI-guided recommendations for robots across every category.

Every robot gets a transparent **RoboScore** — scored on 8 dimensions including performance, reliability, ease of use, and value. The methodology is public. Manufacturers cannot pay to influence scores. This is our core commitment.

The **AI Advisor** (powered by Claude) lets you describe your situation in plain English and get 3 specific recommendations with prices, scores, and direct links to full reviews.

**Why this matters for businesses:**

The global robotics market hits $200B+ this year. Companies are deploying robots faster than they can evaluate them properly. Bad purchases waste money and erode trust in automation programs.

We want to be the trusted research step between "we should look into robots" and "here's the purchase order."

The platform is free to use. No account required to browse and compare. We monetize through affiliate commissions and a Pro tier — never through manufacturer influence.

If you work in operations, logistics, facilities, or manufacturing, I'd love your feedback on what we've built. What information is missing when you evaluate robots today?
