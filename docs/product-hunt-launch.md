## 3. FIRST COMMENT (Founder Story — Pin This)

```
Hey Product Hunt —

I grew up taking apart every toy robot I could find. Not to break them — to understand
how they worked. That curiosity never went away. It just got more expensive.

Two years ago, I was helping a warehouse operations team evaluate AMRs for their
facility. The budget was $350K. The stakes were real — wrong pick and they'd be stuck
with a fleet of expensive paperweights for 5 years.

Here's what I found: the entire buying process was broken.

Manufacturer websites read like marketing brochures. "Industry-leading performance"
and "best-in-class reliability" on every single page. Review sites were pay-to-play —
the companies being reviewed were funding the reviews. Pricing was opaque. Real
deployment data was nonexistent.

This is a $103 billion market. And the people spending $50K-$500K on automation
decisions had no independent, data-driven guide.

So I built one.

Robotomated tracks 975+ robots from 300+ manufacturers. Every robot is scored
independently across 8 weighted dimensions — Performance, Reliability, Ease of Use,
Intelligence, Value, Ecosystem, Safety, and Design. The methodology is public. The
scores are explainable. No manufacturer has ever paid to influence a rating, and none
ever will.

What you get:
— RoboScores on 975+ robots with transparent methodology
— Side-by-side comparison with real specs, not marketing claims
— 5-year TCO calculators with maintenance, training, and integration costs
— 140+ research articles across 17 industry categories
— AI advisor that recommends based on your actual use case and budget
— Weekly newsletter (The Brief) with market intelligence

The long-term vision is bigger. We're building the Workforce Network — connecting
operations teams who deploy robots with the training, maintenance, and integration
partners they need. The intelligence layer for the entire robotics buying journey.

We bootstrapped this on $10K. No VC. No manufacturer deals. Just data and
editorial independence.

Three things I'd love feedback on from the PH community:

1. **Scoring transparency** — We publish our full methodology. Is the 8-dimension
   breakdown clear enough? Would you trust a RoboScore when evaluating a purchase?

2. **Compare experience** — The side-by-side comparison pulls real specs, not
   marketing summaries. What additional data points would help your evaluation?

3. **AI Advisor** — It recommends robots based on use case, budget, and facility
   size. How can we make the intake smarter without making it longer?

Thanks for checking us out. Happy to answer anything.

— Apar
```
