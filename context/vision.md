# Robotomated — Vision

## The Opportunity
We are at the inflection point of consumer robotics. Humanoid robots,
service robots, educational bots, and AI-powered home assistants are
shipping with 6-figure unit volumes. Yet consumers, businesses, and
institutions have NO trusted, consolidated resource to navigate the
category. The last time this happened was smartphones in 2007. That
gap produced The Verge, GSMArena, and vertical SaaS worth hundreds
of billions. Robotics is larger — and the platform layer hasn't been built.

## What Robotomated Is
The "Consumer Reports + Amazon + YouTube" of the robotics era.
Five modules, one platform:
1. RoboIntel — AI purchase advisory engine (Claude-powered)
2. RoboReview — Expert + community reviews with RoboScore
3. RoboMarket — Buy/sell/trade marketplace
4. RoboLearn — Education, tutorials, certification
5. RoboConnect — Developer/enterprise APIs and integrations

## The Strategic Reframe
The PRD says enterprise-first at scale. But the founder thinks
consumer-naturally, has $10K, and wants all industries. Resolution:
Build the definitive robotics CONTENT platform that also serves
enterprises. Content first → audience → monetization → enterprise.

## The Moat That Matters
Editorial independence and review quality. If you ever take money
to influence a review score, you've destroyed everything. The Wirecutter
was worth $30M to the NYT because of 10 years of honest reviews.
RoboScore methodology is PUBLIC. Scoring is EXPLAINABLE. "We were
wrong" moments are published LOUDLY. This is what Amazon, manufacturers,
and consultancies cannot replicate.

## Five Structural Moats
1. Data network effect — every review, transaction, and conversation improves the product
2. Editorial trust — years to build, cannot be bought
3. SEO moat — 10,000+ high-value pages = $5M/year equivalent ad spend
4. Marketplace liquidity — once buyers/sellers are on, switching cost is high
5. AI training advantage — proprietary behavioral data continuously improves the advisor
